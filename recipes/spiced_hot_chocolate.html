Spiced Hot Chocolate

A warm and spicy chocolate drink, its origins dating back to ancient Mexico's civilizations the Aztecs and Mayans

This decadent drink is for the nights that are too frigid to step foot outside but that nagging craving for a cold chocolate dessert won't fade away. Instead of a cold dessert this thick and warm hot chocolate is the perfect substitute for satisfying that chocolate craving. The recipe is fairly simple and can be made using ingredients that are commonly found in one's pantry. Spices such as cinnamon, cayenne pepper, and nutmeg along with whole milk or your milk of choice and lastly, cocoa powder. The milk and spices are then whisked in a saucepan and once simmering it is ready.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups milk or milk of choice
  • 1/4 cup Dutch-processed unsweetened cocoa powder (or the cocoa powder of your choice)
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 tsp cornstarch (optional thickener)
  • 1 tsp. cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp. vanilla extract
  • 1/4 tsp. chipotle powder or chili powder
  • 1/4 tsp. vanilla extract
  • pinch of salt
  • pinch of nutmeg
  • pinch of cayenne powder
  • optional toppings: whipped cream, marshmallows, chocolate syrup, and/or chocolate shavings

Steps

  1. Bring milk and cinnamon to a simmer in a medium saucepan over medium-low heat, whisking occasionally and making sure milk doesn't boil, until cinnamon is floral and fragrant, about 10 minutes.
  2. Whisk in cocoa powder, vanilla extract, salt, nutmeg, and cayenne, whisking frequently, until mixture is smooth and creamy and chocolate is melted, about 5 minutes.
  3. Pour into mug and add optional toppings.
